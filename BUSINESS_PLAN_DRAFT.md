# Business Plan Draft (v1)

Built by nerds, for nerds.

## Core Idea

Build open-source apps and retro PC games, then monetize convenience and execution: hosted versions, signed binaries, premium assets, and a small number of high-fit service engagements.

- Brand line: `I build apps. Open code, official builds, real docs, real shipping.`
- Long-term target: services become secondary; products and audience become primary revenue.

## 1) What You Are Building

- Apps: open-source code (AGPLv3), free self-host option, paid hosted option.
- Games: open gameplay code where practical, paid binaries plus paid art/audio/assets.
- Content engine: blog + newsletter as your distribution and trust layer.
- Community: sponsorware early access, public roadmap, public changelog, contributor-friendly repos.

## 2) Who You Serve

- Primary audience: vibecoders, indie devs, technical founders, small teams.
- Client profile (services): teams with a clear problem, budget, and timeline.
- Not for: low-budget vague projects and open-ended agency-style work.

## 3) Revenue Model

### Primary (future)

- Paid hosting for open-source apps
- Paid official binaries/installers
- Paid premium asset packs (especially for games)

### Secondary (now/runway)

- Productized services (fixed scope, limited slots)

### Community support

- GitHub Sponsors/Patreon for early access and supporter perks

### Checkout stack

- Stripe: hosting/services
- Lemon Squeezy: downloadable binaries/assets
- GitHub Sponsors/Patreon: community support

## 4) Offers (Productized)

- Codebase Checkup (entry)
- Build Sprint (core, 2-4 weeks)
- Automation Support (monthly, limited seats)

Rules:

- fixed scope
- fixed outcome
- clear timeline
- application-only intake
- publish "good fit / not fit"

## 5) Distribution Plan (Main Growth Lever)

Website is your core channel.

Weekly cadence:

- 1 real blog post (build log, lesson, or technical guide)
- 1 newsletter issue from that post
- 3 short distribution posts pointing back to your site

Content mix:

- 60% build logs and lessons
- 30% problem-specific technical guides
- 10% proof posts (metrics, case notes, release recaps)

## 6) SEO Plan (From Current Data)

Current problem:

- impressions exist
- CTR is low
- message is inconsistent

Fix sequence:

1. Tighten homepage + page intent (one clear CTA priority)
2. Rewrite top-impression pages for CTR (title/meta/opening)
3. Noindex or rewrite AI-tone low-trust posts
4. Internal links from posts -> app pages -> newsletter -> work page

Site CTA hierarchy:

- Primary: signup/follow updates
- Secondary: use app / join waitlist
- Tertiary: apply to work with me

## 7) 12-Month Milestones

### Q1 (0-3 months)

- lock brand voice + site structure
- launch/refresh `work-with-me` page
- add signup CTA on every blog post
- publish 12 high-quality posts

### Q2 (4-6 months)

- ship at least 1 serious app offer (hosted or paid binary)
- launch sponsorware policy (clear release window)
- build weekly newsletter rhythm

### Q3 (7-9 months)

- expand product catalog (2nd app or game release)
- reduce service dependency

### Q4 (10-12 months)

- products + community revenue offset a meaningful share of service income
- keep services selective and premium

## 8) KPI Dashboard

Audience:

- email subscribers
- returning visitors
- newsletter open/click rate

Product:

- app installs/activations
- waitlist growth
- hosted conversions

Revenue:

- MRR from hosting
- monthly binary/asset sales
- service revenue share (target trend: down over time)

SEO:

- organic clicks
- CTR on top pages
- pages in top 10 positions

## 9) Risks and Controls

- Risk: too many projects, weak focus
  - Control: feature only "active this month" projects on homepage.
- Risk: AI-tone content hurts trust
  - Control: rewrite/noindex weak posts and add proof blocks.
- Risk: services consume all time
  - Control: hard cap service slots and protect product build time.

## 10) First 30 Days

- Finalize homepage CTA hierarchy and consistent hero pattern.
- Add blog-post CTA module (signup primary).
- Launch `work-with-me` with strict qualification form.
- Audit all blog posts into keep/rewrite/noindex.
- Publish 4 strong no-BS posts tied to real builds.
- Wire Netlify forms to SendGrid and start weekly sends.
